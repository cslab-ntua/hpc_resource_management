# Welcome to Our Research Team

High Performance Computing (HPC) clusters are often used to solve complex problems across a wide range of research domains, such as weather modeling and human genome sequencing. Efficient job scheduling in these systems is a critical factor for achieving effective resource utilization and energy savings. The core software components of such systems include the resource manager and the job scheduler. The former is responsible for allocating resources, while the latter decides when and where jobs will be executed. A common objective across research directions in this field is the improvement of decision-making by the job scheduler.
